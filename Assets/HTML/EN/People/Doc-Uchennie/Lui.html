Origin

Louis Pasteur was born on December 27, 1822 in Dou, a small town in the Jura region of France. He was the third child in a family of tanners.

As a child, Louis Pasteur was a very smart and talented child who loved to study nature and experiment. When he was 9 years old, his family moved to the city of Arles (Arbois), where Louis began to study at a local school.

Start

Louis Pasteur began his scientific career in 1847 when he was appointed professor of physics at the University of Lille in France. He was engaged in the study of crystals and optics, and popularized scientific knowledge among students and the public.

Pasteur continued his scientific career in 1854 when he was asked to take up the problem of wine spoilage in France. He conducted a series of experiments and found that spoilage occurs due to the presence of microorganisms in the wine, which cause fermentation and rotting. Pasteur proposed a method of pasteurization which consisted of heating wine to a certain temperature in order to kill micro-organisms and thus keep the wine fresh.

World famous

After success with the pasteurization method, Pasteur began to research microbiology and bacteriology, and made a number of important discoveries in this area. He showed that certain bacteria were capable of causing disease in humans and animals, and he developed methods to combat infectious diseases such as rabies and plague.

Having completed his study of fermentation and disease in silkworms, Pasteur turned to the study of anthrax, in the study of the causes of which there was little progress by the mid-1870s. The scientist used the technique of the German microbiologist Robert Koch - successively subcultured bacterial cultures with subsequent weakening of the concentration. Having isolated the culture of the pathogen, Pasteur reseeded it several times; in this case, the culture of the bacteria settled to the bottom of the flask, while the clear liquid at the top was harmless when administered to the experimental animals. Louis Pasteur was able to prove that almost a few bacteria can kill an animal. In addition, he proved that the burial of infected animals spreads the causative agent of the epidemic through earthworms. Thus, Pasteur, having neither medical nor biological education, devoted himself to immunology.

At that time, there was a method of inoculating a weakened form of the disease of only one disease to develop immunity - smallpox. This method is called "vaccination". Pasteur used it as a common noun for any form of immunization. Louis Pasteur was the first to apply an ecological approach to microbiology, since he believed that there was a direct relationship between living conditions and the composition of the environment: turning to the problems of chicken cholera, he used chicken broth; earlier, when working with yeast, he used solutions where he added yeast ash.

In 1885, Pasteur was invited to the Pasteur Institute in Paris, where he continued his research and developed a vaccine against rabies. This vaccine was one of the most important medical discoveries in history and saved many lives.

In addition, Pasteur made many other discoveries and inventions, including methods of sterilization, the study of the acidity of milk and related methods of cheese production, and the development of methods for the production of vaccines against other diseases.

Thanks to his research and discoveries, Louis Pasteur became one of the greatest scientists in history and his contribution to science and medicine is still invaluable.

End of Path

Louis Pasteur continued to work throughout his life and made great scientific progress. He continued to research microbiology and bacteriology, and made many discoveries in these areas.

Pasteur died on 28 September 1895 at his home in Marseille at the age of 72. He was buried in the crypt of the Pasteur Institute in Paris, which was founded in 1887.

After his death, the Pasteur Institute continued to develop and became one of the most famous and prestigious scientific centers in the world. Today, more than 100 years after the death of Louis Pasteur, his scientific discoveries and methods continue to have a huge impact on medicine, agriculture, the food industry and other areas of people's lives.

Louis Pasteur left a huge scientific legacy that still inspires scientists around the world. His name has become a symbol of science and innovation, and he remains one of the greatest scientists in history.
